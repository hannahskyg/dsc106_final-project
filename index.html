Did people get richer after COVID!?

US Households' Financial Situation Pre- and Post-COVID Across Different Demographics

By: Hannah Gonzalez, Edwards Tong, Janie Chan, and Junran Yang

Wealth is Growing.. or is it?

Between 2019 and 2022, the average American household saw their net worth increase. The overall mean household net worth increased significantly.

But... Not Everyone Benefits Equally

When we break down the data by the sex of the household head, we see disparities. Looking at the change, female households have a smaller change in networth than males households.

Age Tells a Different Story

Looking at age groups reveals an even more complex picture. Middle-aged households (ages 45-64) hold significantly more wealth than younger or older households.

Young households (under 35) have dramatically lower net worth, while older households (65+) show moderate levelsâ€”likely reflecting retirement asset drawdowns.

Education, the Problem

When examining education levels, households headed by someone with someone of a high school degree or less had less networth.

Therefore... Inequality Persists

While the overall picture shows growth, the benefits of economic expansion are unevenly distributed across demographics. Gender, age, and education create significant wealth divides.

Understanding these patterns is crucial for addressing financial inequality in America.
